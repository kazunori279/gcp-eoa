For Workshop Organizers & Speakers

  • Pick any workshop content to fit your requirements and expectations from the audience.
  • Using Qwiklabs? Check "Getting Started with Qwiklabs". The "Generative AI Explorer" course will download all notebooks available here.
  • Speakers are responsible for checking that notebooks work in the lab environment — credits, quota limits, IAM, and especially availability of GPU and TPU resources. If you see any issues or questions, please contact the content owners.
  • Collecting feedback: please copy and use the feedback form for collecting feedback from participants.
  • Tier 1/2 or online events: please contact Lavi Nigam or Kaz Sato for coordination and support.
  • If you feel we need to add slides/materials for a specific workshop, please let us know!
  • If you need new workshop material or want to use materials in the parking lot, please let us know!

For Content Owners

Content owners are expected to:

  • Be the owner: Update each workshop's columns and be the point of contact for each workshop material.
  • Nominate speaker candidates: Ideally cover different timezones. Add the name to the "Speaker" column.
  • Choose and maintain "workshop-ready" notebooks/materials that are:
    • Self-contained as textbooks: Include not only code cells but also instruction texts, diagrams, videos (if needed), or links to external demos. Speakers and audience should be able to learn the topic as smoothly as reading a book.
    • "Live" and maintained: Works with the latest models, APIs, and SDKs.
    • Conforming with the notebook style guide.
    • Portable: Supports Colab Enterprise and Workbench, works with Qwiklab projects and their limited quota/resources, and written in easy English for non-English audiences.
  • Set the status to "Ready" once you confirm everything is updated and ready to be used for the workshop.
  • Add new workshop ideas to the parking lot area and let Lavi Nigam or Kaz Sato know!
